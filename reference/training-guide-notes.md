# Apollo & Artemis Training Guide — Key Notes

Extracted from the full training guide PDF (also included in this project).

## Breed & Age
- Pit bull × border collie mix
- ~5-6 months old at plan start
- Intelligence: 10/10. Energy: 10/10. Sensitivity: 9/10.
- 50-70 lbs as adults
- Full maturity: 18-24 months away
- Cap exercise at 25 min/session (bones still growing)
- Puppies need 14-16 hours of sleep/day

## Critical Rules (Non-Negotiable)
1. Train them one at a time — always
2. Walk them separately at least 3x/week
3. Each dog sleeps in her own crate
4. Each dog gets 10-15 min alone with Diana daily
5. Always feed in separate spots

## Female-Female Risk
Two female dogs raised together may fight seriously as they mature. The separation rules are essential, not optional.

## Apollo — Reactive/Fearful
- She is SCARED, not aggressive
- Punishing fear makes it worse and increases bite risk
- Desensitization: scary thing appears → high-value treats (chicken) fast and continuously → scary thing leaves → treats stop
- Never force her toward triggers
- Track: what trigger, how close, reaction level 1-5
- May need anxiety medication — not giving up, giving training a better chance
- Yellow bandana or "NERVOUS" vest recommended
- Does NOT have to greet guests — safe retreat always available

## Artemis — PSD Pathway
- Confident, calmer sister
- Better service dog candidate
- Don't make final PSD decision until 12-18 months
- PSD readiness signs: focused on Diana, bounces back from surprises, calm in public, tolerates handling
- Foundation skills before tasks: sustained focus, settle, under, leave food, stranger neutrality
- Possible tasks: deep pressure therapy, interruption, room check, grounding, medication reminder
- Wait until 18-24 months for spaying (large breed)

## Equipment
- Wire crates 42" (2, never shared)
- Front-clip harnesses (Ruffwear or Julius-K9)
- 6-foot leashes (no retractable)
- 15-30 foot long leash for recall
- Baby gates (2+)
- Puzzle feeders, snuffle mats, frozen Kongs
- Treat pouch, pea-sized treats
- NO choke/prong/shock collars

## Training Session Rules
- 5-10 minutes per session
- Always end on a win
- Two short sessions > one long one
- Don't train when overstimulated, too hungry, or post-exercise
- Going badly → stop, breathe, start simple

## Luis's Role
- At least one training session per day
- At least one solo walk per week with each dog
- Same commands, same rules as Diana
