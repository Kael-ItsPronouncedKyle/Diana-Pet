## Your First Time Opening the App

When you open the app for the first time, it will walk you through a few steps:

1. **Welcome screen** — Just a hello!
2. **Pick your creature** — Choose from six friends: Puppy 🐶, Kitty 🐱, Angel 👼, Dragon 🐲, Bunny 🐰, or Fox 🦊
3. **Name your creature** — Give them any name you want.
4. **Meet your creature** — They will say hi and tell you a bit about themselves.
5. **Optional: thought screening** — You can turn on a weekly check about your thoughts. You can skip this. You can always turn it on later in Settings.

That's it! You're ready to go.

## Meet Your Creature

Your creature lives at the top of the Home screen. They are your little buddy.

### How Your Creature Works

Your creature gets happier based on **how many check-ins you do** — not what you report. A hard day with lots of check-ins = a glowing creature. That's on purpose. Showing up is what matters.

| Check-ins done | How your creature feels |
|---|---|
| 0 | Sleeping ☁️ |
| 1 | Bouncing — happy to see you! 🌟 |
| 2–3 | Wiggling — you're doing great together! 💚 |
| 4+ | Glowing — shining bright! ✨✨✨ |

Your creature also reacts when you do specific things:
- Log sleep → they yawn
- Drink water → they take a sip
- Practice a DBT skill → they meditate
- Log puppy training → they wag
- Log meals, meds, or wins → they celebrate

### Creature Personalities

Each creature talks differently based on the time of day. For example:
- **Puppy** wags in the morning, offers belly rubs at midday, curls up at night.
- **Dragon** breathes warm fire in the morning and guards you in the afternoon.
- **Fox** scouts ahead in the morning and settles into their den at night.

They all love you. They just show it in their own way.

## The Home Screen

The Home screen changes based on what time of day it is. This helps you know what to check in on right now.

### Morning (before noon)
The app will suggest:
- **Sleep** — How did you sleep?
- **Morning meds** — Did you take them?
- **Energy** — How's your battery today?
- **Window** — How's your stress level?
- **Feelings** — What are you feeling?
- **Word of the Day** — A new word to learn!

### Midday (noon to 5pm)
The app will suggest:
- **Water** — Have you been drinking water?
- **Puppy training** — Did you train today?
- **DBT skill** — Try a coping skill.
- **Energy check** — How's your battery now?

### Evening (after 5pm)
The app will suggest:
- **Circles** — How was your day overall?
- **Feelings** — What are you feeling tonight?
- **Evening meds** — Did you take them?
- **Sensory check** — Are your senses overwhelmed?
- **Present check** — Do you feel grounded?
- **Body check** — Do you feel connected to your body?

### Late Night (9pm – 4am)
The screen gets darker and calmer. Your creature watches over you. The crisis toolkit is always one tap away.

### Other Things on the Home Screen

- **Streak counter** — Shows how many days in a row you've checked in.
- **Water counter** — Tap to add a glass. Goal is 8.
- **Meds quick log** — Quick tap to mark meds as taken.
- **Milestones** — At 3, 7, 12, and 17 check-ins you get a celebration with confetti! 🎉

## Check-Ins

Tap the **Checkins** tab to see all your check-in options in a tile grid. There are three groups.

Every check-in is:
- **Quick** — Under 2 minutes each
- **Tap-based** — Mostly tapping, very little typing
- **Skippable** — You never have to fill in text fields if you don't want to
- **No wrong answers** — Hard days get warmth, not punishment

## Heart & Recovery Check-Ins

These help you track your feelings, urges, and recovery work.

### Circles (Three Circles)
Rate your day using three circles:
- 🟢 **Outer (green)** — A healing day. You stayed in your healthy zone.
- 🟡 **Middle (yellow)** — A mixed day. Some warning signs.
- 🔴 **Inner (red)** — A hard day. You acted on urges.

After you pick, you can add a short note about what happened. There's no judgment here. Every honest answer helps.

**Secrecy test:** Sometimes after an inner circle day, the app will gently ask: "Is there something you're not saying?" This is a nudge, not a demand. Honesty with yourself is part of recovery.

### Feelings (Emotion Wheel)
Pick up to 5 emotions from four groups. If you're not sure what you feel, tap "I don't know" — that's a real answer.

After picking, you can:
- Say what started the feeling
- Point to where you feel it in your body (head, chest, stomach, etc.)
- Read a short definition of the emotion — this builds your feeling-words over time

### DBT Skill
DBT stands for Dialectical Behavior Therapy (a way to handle big feelings). The app has 20+ skills you can learn and practice.

Each skill shows:
- **What it is** — A simple explanation
- **How to practice it** — Step-by-step instructions

Skills are sorted into three groups:
- **Mindfulness** — Paying attention to the present moment
- **Distress Tolerance** — Getting through a crisis without making it worse
- **Emotion Regulation** — Understanding and managing big feelings

The app will sometimes suggest a skill based on what you're going through. Tap it to mark it as practiced.

### Urges
Log when you feel an urge. You can track:
- What kind of urge
- How strong it was (1–5)
- How long it lasted

The next morning, the app may ask: "You had urges yesterday. How did you handle it?" This helps you reflect and learn.

### Chain Analysis
This is a deeper look at what happened when you had a hard moment. The app walks you through:

1. **What made you vulnerable?** (bad sleep, missed meds, loneliness, pain, etc.)
2. **What set it off?** (the thing that happened right before)
3. **What did you feel and think?**
4. **What did you do?**
5. **What happened after?**

This is not about blame. It's about understanding patterns so you can catch them earlier next time.

### Connection
Track who you connected with today:
- Who was it?
- Did you feel close to them?
- Did you laugh together?

Connection matters for recovery. The app tracks this over time and may notice patterns — like that you have more green-circle days when you feel close to someone.

## Body & Brain Check-Ins

These help you track your physical health and how your body feels.

### Sleep
- When did you go to bed?
- When did you wake up?
- How many times did you wake up at night?
- How was your sleep quality? (rate 1–5 with emoji faces)

If your sleep was rough, the app will show a sleep tip to try tonight.

**Important:** If you slept very little but feel high energy, the app will gently flag this. Very low sleep + high energy can be an early sign of mania (when your mood goes too high). This flag is there to help you catch it early.

### Meds
Quick tap for morning and evening meds:
- ✅ **Yes** — Took them
- ⏳ **Not yet** — Haven't taken them yet
- ❌ **Don't have** — Can't take them today

The app tracks your medication streak (how many days in a row). If you miss 2+ days, it will check in about it — not to scold you, but because your meds matter.

### Meals
Track breakfast, lunch, and dinner with a simple tap. You can add the time if you want. The week view will show your average meals per day.

### Energy
Rate your energy from 1 to 5. This helps track your ME/CFS crashes and overall battery level through the day.

### Water
Tap to add a glass of water. The goal is 8 glasses. You get a little celebration when you hit it! 💧

### Window (Stress Response)
This checks how your nervous system is doing:
- 🟢 **Green** — Calm. You feel okay.
- 🟡 **Yellow** — Activated. You feel revved up or on edge.
- 🔴 **Red** — Flooded. You feel overwhelmed or shut down.

This is also called your "window of tolerance" — the range where you can handle things. It's okay to be outside it. The app just helps you notice.

### Sensory Check
Rate how overwhelmed your senses feel (1–5). Lights too bright? Sounds too loud? Skin too sensitive? This tracks your sensory load over time.

### Present? (Dissociation Check)
This checks if you feel grounded and here, or if you feel disconnected, floaty, or far away. If you're feeling disconnected, you can say how strong the feeling is.

Feeling disconnected sometimes is normal. Tracking it helps you see patterns.

### Body Check
Do you feel connected to your body today? A simple check-in about how in touch you feel with yourself physically.

### Weekly Screening
If you turned on the thought screening in Settings, this appears once a week. It asks a few questions about unusual thoughts or experiences. This is private and just for your own tracking.

## Life Building Check-Ins

These track the good stuff you're building in your life.

### Wins
Write down something that went well today! Big or small. "I drank all my water." "I told the truth when it was hard." "I pet my dog." All wins count.

Your wins show up in the Week view so you can look back at them.

### Reading
Track how many minutes you read or listened to an audiobook today. The Week view shows your total for the week.

### My Trackers
These are custom trackers you create yourself. You pick an emoji and a name, and then toggle them on or off each day. You can have up to 12.

To set these up, go to **Settings → Custom Trackers**.

## The Crisis Toolkit

The red button is always on screen. Always. Every tab, every time. One tap opens the Crisis Toolkit.

The toolkit has six sections:

### 🌱 Quick Calm
Two exercises to help right now:

- **Box Breathing** — Breathe in for 4 counts, hold for 4, out for 4, hold for 4. The app shows a visual timer with colors to follow.
- **5-4-3-2-1 Grounding** — Find 5 things you can see, 4 you can hear, 3 you can feel, 2 you can smell, 1 you can taste. Brings you back to the present moment.

### 🛡️ My Safety Plan
Your personal safety plan. If you haven't set it up yet, the app will walk you through it. It includes:
- Warning signs (how you know things are getting bad)
- Things you can do on your own to cope
- People and places that can distract you
- People you can call for help
- Professional contacts
- How to make your space safer

You can set up or edit your safety plan in **Settings → Safety Plan Setup**.

### 💪 Coping Skills
Your personal coping plan — things YOU wrote for yourself. Six prompts:
1. When I feel an urge, I will...
2. When I can't sleep, I will...
3. When I'm in sensory overload, I will...
4. When I feel disconnected from my body, I will...
5. When I want to hide something, I will...
6. The reason I'm doing this work is...

Fill these in when you're feeling okay. Read them when you're not.

### 💌 Letter from Me
A letter you write to yourself. Write it on a good day. Read it on a hard one. Only you can see it.

### 💚 Words from Kael
Messages from Kael — your person. When you need to hear something steady and loving, this is where to go. Your creature will share these words with you.

### 📞 Call Someone
Quick access to your contacts. One tap to call. This is the last section on purpose — the toolkit teaches you to try skills first, then reach out if you still need to.

## Puppy Training

The Puppies tab tracks training for Apollo and Artemis.

### About the Dogs
- **Apollo** 🐕 — Reactive and fearful. He gets scared, not bad. Training focuses on building his confidence slowly.
- **Artemis** 🐕 — Confident and calm. She's the PSD (Psychiatric Service Dog) candidate. Training builds on her natural strengths.

### How Training Works

Training is split into three phases:

**Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–4)**
Basic skills like name game, sit, down, stay, come, leash walking, crate practice, and more. 18 skills total.

**Phase 2 — Real Skills (Weeks 5–10)**
Shared skills plus dog-specific work:
- Apollo: quiet walks, getting used to scary things slowly, trigger logging
- Artemis: touch command, calm dog exposure

**Phase 3 — Real World (Weeks 11–16)**
Practice in public, with different people handling the dogs, and checking how they do with real-world challenges.

### Daily Training Features
- **8 rotating skills** shown each day (a different mix each time)
- **Tap to mark done** — ✅ means done, ⭕ means not yet
- **Pick the trainer** — Diana, Luis, or Both
- **Apollo's trigger log** — Track what scared him, how far away it was, and how he reacted (1–5)
- **Training notes** — Add notes about the session. You can use voice-to-text!
- **Rotating tips** — A new training tip shows up regularly

### Moving to the Next Phase
When you've been in a phase long enough (minimum number of weeks), a button will appear to move to the next one.

## Your Week View

The **Week** tab shows your last 7 days at a glance.

### The 7-Day Grid
Each day shows:
- A colored circle (green, yellow, or red) matching your Circles rating
- Small icons for urges, meds, DBT skills, and water
- A symbol: ✓ (good day), ~ (mixed), ! (hard day)

### Patterns
The app looks at your week and spots patterns. These show up as messages from your creature, like "Things I noticed 🔍."

Patterns are color-coded:
- 🔴 **Red** — Safety-level concern (like missed meds + substance use together)
- 🟠 **Orange** — Something to watch
- 🟡 **Yellow** — Worth noticing
- 🟢 **Green** — Good things happening
- 🔵 **Blue** — Fun facts and encouragement

### Weekly Recaps
At the bottom of the Week view, you'll find:
- **Wins Recap** — All your wins listed by day
- **Reading Recap** — Total minutes read this week
- **Connection Recap** — How many days you connected, felt close, or laughed
- **Meal Recap** — Average meals per day (with a gentle note if it's under 2)

## Settings

Tap the gear icon (top left) to open Settings. Here's what you'll find:

| Setting | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Safety Plan Setup** | Walk through setting up or editing your safety plan |
| **Provider Export** | Create a summary of your last 7 days to share with your therapist |
| **Creature Selection** | Change your creature or their name |
| **Crisis Contacts** | Set up quick-call numbers |
| **Display & Feel** | Dark mode and haptics (vibration feedback) on/off |
| **Puppy Training** | Pick which training phase you're in |
| **Custom Trackers** | Add or remove your own tracking items |
| **Luis Shift Calendar** | Mark which days Luis works |
| **Thought Screening** | Turn the weekly thought screening on or off |
| **Reset All Data** | Erase everything and start over (asks you twice to be sure) |

### Dark Mode
The app can switch to darker colors that are easier on your eyes. It can detect evening automatically, or you can toggle it yourself in Settings.

### Provider Export
This creates a summary of your week that you can share with your therapist or counselor. It includes check-in patterns, mood trends, and anything important — without sharing your private journal entries.

## Tips & Tricks

### Swipe Between Tabs
You can swipe left or right to move between tabs. Or tap the icons at the bottom.

### Voice-to-Text
Any time you see a text box (journal entries, training notes, etc.), you can use your phone's voice-to-text instead of typing. Just tap the microphone on your keyboard.

### Undo Actions
Some quick actions (like adding water) have a 5-second undo. If you tap by accident, you can take it back.

### Word of the Day
Each morning, the app shows a new word with its meaning and an example sentence. These are recovery and feelings words. Over time, you'll build a bigger vocabulary for talking about how you feel. The Week view tracks how many words you've learned.

### Streaks
Your check-in streak counts how many days in a row you've done at least one check-in. If you miss a day, it resets to 1 on your next check-in. Don't stress about it — just show up today.

### The App Works Offline
Once you've loaded the app, it works without internet. Your data is saved on your phone. You don't need WiFi to check in.

### Everything is Private
Your data stays on your device. The app doesn't send your check-ins anywhere unless you choose to export them (like with Provider Export).

## Quick Reference: What to Do When...

| When you feel... | Try this |
|---|---|
| **Overwhelmed** | Crisis Toolkit → Quick Calm → Box Breathing |
| **Disconnected or floaty** | Crisis Toolkit → Quick Calm → 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding |
| **An urge** | Log it in Urges → Read your Coping Plan → Try a DBT Skill |
| **Like hiding something** | Read your Coping Plan prompt #5 → Try the Secrecy Test |
| **Really low energy** | Log Energy → Rest. Rest is treatment, not laziness. |
| **Sensory overload** | Log Sensory → Read your Coping Plan prompt #3 → Find a quiet space |
| **Sad or confused about feelings** | Feelings check-in → "I don't know" is a valid answer |
| **Like you need to talk to someone** | Crisis Toolkit → Call Someone |
| **Proud of yourself** | Log it in Wins! Every win counts. |
