# Diana — Profile Reference

This document exists so the builder understands who they are building for. Every design choice in the app flows from this.

## The Basics

- **Age:** 20
- **Location:** College Station / Bryan, Texas
- **Gender:** Transgender woman
- **Relationship:** Married to Luis (25), polyamorous
- **Cultural background:** Mesoamerican, Catholic, some Pentecostal influence, exploring Kabbalah
- **Insurance:** BCBS TX STAR Kids Medicaid (#525616526)

## Clinical Picture

- **Autism + ADHD (AuDHD):** Needs predictable structure, sensory-aware environments, visual supports, tap-based interfaces. Gets overwhelmed by walls of text and unpredictable layouts.
- **Bipolar 1:** Sleep disruption is the #1 early warning sign for manic episodes. Medication compliance is critical. Hypersexuality during manic episodes is clinically linked to her compulsive sexual behavior diagnosis.
- **Possible schizoaffective:** Not confirmed. Screening module included in app but hidden by default. Can be activated.
- **ME/CFS (Chronic Fatigue Syndrome):** Real, debilitating energy crashes. Activity tolerance varies wildly day to day. Rest is treatment. Overexertion triggers multi-day crashes.
- **Compulsive sexual behavior:** In active treatment. Uses the SAA Three Circles model (Outer = healthy, Middle = warning, Inner = acted out). Recovery documents center her own growth, not the relationship.
- **Reading level:** Approximately 3rd grade. Actively learning to read. Processes written language differently. Does NOT reflect her intellectual capacity. She is an intelligent adult.

## What Matters for the App

### Language
- 3rd grade reading level for all UI text
- Warm, direct, emotionally honest
- Never condescending — never clinical
- Define harder words naturally inline
- Short sentences, clear structure

### Interaction
- Tap-based everything
- Voice-to-text compatible for journal/notes
- All text input is optional and skippable
- Under 2 minutes per check-in section
- Predictable layouts — same format every time

### Emotional Safety
- Hard days are NEVER punished
- The creature responds to engagement, not to reported values
- Affirming language on every difficult check-in
- Crisis toolkit always one tap away
- Recovery is centered on HER growth — never framed around Luis's experience

### Clinical Priorities
1. Sleep tracking (bipolar management)
2. Medication tracking (bipolar + schizoaffective management)
3. Three Circles + urge logging (compulsive sexual behavior recovery)
4. Energy + crash tracking (ME/CFS management)
5. Sensory load tracking (autism support)
6. Mania early warning (bipolar monitoring)
7. DBT skills practice (emotional regulation)

## The People Around Her

- **Luis:** Her husband. 25. On-the-ground support. Rotating shift schedule (Thu-Sun). Also training the dogs. Described as having no other support network.
- **Apollo & Artemis:** Her two pit bull × border collie puppies (sisters, ~6 months old). Apollo is reactive/fearful. Artemis is the PSD candidate. They follow a 16-week force-free training plan.

## The Non-Negotiables

1. Diana is an intelligent adult. The app never talks down to her.
2. Bad days get warmth, not punishment.
3. The crisis toolkit is always accessible. One tap. Every screen.
4. Recovery is hers. Not about Luis, not about anyone else.
5. This app is built with love by someone who cares about her. Build it like you care too.
